# Paco's Taco Shop Operations Manual v4.2

**Effective Date:** June 1, 2026  
**Audience:** Franchise Owners, General Managers, Shift Leaders  
**Confidential — For Authorized Franchise Personnel Only**

---

## 1. Purpose & Philosophy

Paco's Taco Shop exists to serve **authentic Mexican food made fresh daily** in a fast-casual environment where every guest feels like family. This manual defines the non-negotiable standards that protect our brand, food quality, and guest experience across all franchise locations.

Our operating philosophy rests on four pillars:

1. **Freshness First** — Proteins marinated in-house, salsas made daily, tortillas warmed to order.
2. **Speed with Soul** — Target ticket times under 6 minutes without sacrificing hospitality.
3. **Consistent Execution** — Recipes, portions, and plating match corporate specifications.
4. **Profitable Operations** — Labor, food cost, and waste managed to franchise benchmarks.

---

## 2. Organizational Structure

### 2.1 Recommended Staffing Model (Annual Volume $1.0M–$1.3M)

- **Franchise Owner / Operating Partner** — P&L accountability, community presence, hiring
- **General Manager** — Daily operations, scheduling, inventory, training
- **Shift Leaders (2–3)** — Peak coverage, cash handling, station certification
- **Line Crew (8–12)** — Prep, grill, expo, front counter, drive-thru (if applicable)

### 2.2 Labor Targets

- **Total labor:** 28–32% of net sales
- **Management labor:** 8–10% of net sales
- **Peak staffing ratio:** 1 crew member per $120/hour in projected sales

---

## 3. Guest Service Standards

### 3.1 The Paco's Greeting

Every guest must be acknowledged within **10 seconds** of entering the line or approaching the counter.

**Script:** "Welcome to Paco's! What can I start for you today?"

### 3.2 Order Accuracy

- Repeat the order back before finalizing payment
- Confirm modifications and allergens when noted
- Expo verifies every ticket against the order screen before handoff

**Target order accuracy:** 98.5% or higher

### 3.3 Speed of Service

| Daypart | Target Ticket Time |
|---------|-------------------|
| Breakfast (if offered) | Under 5 minutes |
| Lunch peak | Under 6 minutes |
| Dinner peak | Under 7 minutes |

### 3.4 Recovery Standards

When a mistake occurs:

1. Apologize sincerely without blaming the guest
2. Remake the item immediately — no charge for corporate recipe errors
3. Offer a beverage or chips & salsa when wait exceeds 10 minutes
4. Log the incident in the daily manager log

---

## 4. Recipe & Portion Standards

All locations must use **approved corporate recipes**. Local menu price overrides are permitted within ±15% of corporate pricing without pre-approval.

### 4.1 Core Portion Guide

| Item | Standard Portion |
|------|-----------------|
| Street Taco (single) | 2.5 oz protein, 1 corn tortilla |
| Burrito | 6 oz protein, 12" flour tortilla, max 8 ingredients |
| Chips & Salsa | 4 oz chips, 3 oz salsa (house or verde) |
| Queso | 4 oz served at 140°F minimum |

### 4.2 Daily Prep Requirements

- **Proteins:** Marinated by 9:00 AM; never held beyond 4 hours in hot holding
- **Salsas:** First batch by 8:00 AM; refreshed every 4 hours
- **Rice & Beans:** Cooked fresh daily; discard at close
- **Guacamole:** Made in batches; 3-hour hold maximum

## 5. Cash Handling & POS

### 5.1 Drawer Accountability

- $150 starting bank per drawer
- Single drawer per register during shift
- Blind drops at $300 excess cash

### 5.2 Discount Authorization

| Discount Type | Max Without GM | GM Approval |
|--------------|----------------|-------------|
| Employee meal | 50% | Auto |
| Guest recovery | 100% item | Shift Leader |
| Marketing promo | Per campaign | Pre-loaded in POS |

### 5.3 Daily Reconciliation

Managers must complete the **Daily Sales & Cash Report** in the back office portal by 11:00 PM local time.

---

## 6. Cleanliness & Maintenance

### 6.1 Cleaning Zones

Assign zone ownership by shift. Deep clean schedules are in the Food Safety & Sanitation Guide.

**Hourly tasks:** Dining room wipe-down, restroom check, trash, line sanitizer buckets

**Daily tasks:** Hood filters check, floor mop, walk-in organization

**Weekly tasks:** Equipment degrease, ceiling vent dusting, dumpster pad wash

### 6.2 Equipment Maintenance Log

Track all refrigeration, grill, and POS repairs. Warranty work must use **approved service vendors** listed in the Inventory & Ordering Guide.

## 9. Compliance & Enforcement

Failure to maintain brand standards may result in:

1. Written notice and corrective action plan
2. Mandatory re-training visit from corporate operations
3. Suspension of local marketing fund co-op matching
4. Termination of franchise agreement for repeated or severe violations

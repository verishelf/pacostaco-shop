# Food Safety & Sanitation Guide

**Effective Date:** May 20, 2026  
**Audience:** All store personnel  
**Compliance:** FDA Food Code, local health department, ServSafe standards

---

## 1. Food Safety Culture

Every team member is a food safety leader. Managers must model and enforce standards without exception. When in doubt, **throw it out**.

---

## 2. Temperature Control

### 2.1 Critical Limits

| Product | Cold Hold | Hot Hold | Cook Temp |
|---------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Chicken (pollo) | ≤ 41°F | ≥ 135°F | 165°F |
| Carne asada | ≤ 41°F | ≥ 135°F | 145°F |
| Al pastor | ≤ 41°F | ≥ 135°F | 165°F |
| Rice | ≤ 41°F | ≥ 135°F | 165°F (reheat) |
| Queso | ≤ 41°F | ≥ 140°F | 165°F (reheat) |
| Guacamole | ≤ 41°F | N/A | Fresh daily |

### 2.2 Temperature Logging

- Log walk-in, line, and hot holding temps **every 4 hours**
- Use calibrated thermometers; calibrate weekly with ice water test
- Any out-of-range reading triggers the **Product Hold Procedure** (Section 6)

---

## 3. Personal Hygiene

All team members must:

1. Wash hands for 20 seconds before food handling, after breaks, and after handling raw protein
2. Wear clean uniforms, non-slip shoes, and hair restraints
3. Remove jewelry (except plain wedding band) before prep
4. Report illness (vomiting, diarrhea, jaundice, sore throat with fever) to manager immediately

**Sick policy:** No food handling until 24 hours after symptoms end.

---

## 4. Cross-Contact & Allergens

### 4.1 Major Allergens on Menu

- **Gluten** — Flour tortillas, beer-battered items, some marinades
- **Dairy** — Queso, crema, cheese blends
- **Soy** — Some marinades and oil blends

### 4.2 Allergen Protocol

When a guest declares an allergy:

1. Manager or certified shift leader takes the order
2. Change gloves and use clean utensils
3. Prepare on sanitized surface away from allergen ingredients
4. Hand deliver with verbal confirmation of modifications

**Never** tell a guest a dish is "probably fine."

---

## 5. Cleaning & Sanitizing

### 5.1 Sanitizer Concentration

- **Quat sanitizer:** 200 ppm (test strips every shift change)
- **Bleach solution:** 100 ppm (if approved by local health authority)

### 5.2 Three-Compartment Sink Procedure

1. Wash with hot soapy water
2. Rinse with clean water
3. Sanitize for 60 seconds; air dry

### 5.3 Daily Sanitation Schedule

| Area | Frequency | Responsible |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| Line surfaces | Every 4 hours | Shift Leader |
| Dining tables | Hourly | Front of house |
| Restrooms | Every 2 hours | Assigned crew |
| Walk-in shelves | Daily | Prep crew |
| Grease traps | Weekly | GM or vendor |

---

## 6. Product Hold & Discard Procedure

When temperature or time limits are exceeded:

1. **Hold** — Label "DO NOT USE" and isolate product
2. **Assess** — Manager determines if product can be rapidly cooled/reheated per FDA guidelines
3. **Discard** — If not correctable within 30 minutes, log waste and dispose
4. **Document** — Record in waste log with reason code

---

## 7. Pest Control

- Monthly service by **Terminix Franchise Program** (corporate contract)
- Report any droppings, gnaw marks, or live pests within 1 hour to vendor and corporate
- Never use store-bought pesticides in food areas

---

## 8. Health Inspection Readiness

### 8.1 Pre-Inspection Checklist (Weekly)

- Thermometer calibration log current
- Sanitizer test strips in date
- Employee food handler cards posted and current
- Chemical storage labeled and separated from food
- Handwash stations stocked (soap, paper towels, signage)

### 8.2 Target Score

**90 or above** on health inspections. Scores below 85 require a corrective action plan within 48 hours.

---

## 9. Emergency Procedures

| Event | Immediate Action |
|-------|-----------------|
| Power outage | Transfer proteins to backup cooler; log times |
| Water boil advisory | Use bottled water for prep and ice |
| Chemical spill | Evacuate area; follow SDS sheet |
| Guest illness complaint | Preserve sample if possible; contact corporate within 2 hours |

---

*Food safety is not optional. Violations may result in immediate closure and franchise review.*
